Dal Baati Churma

A Rajasthani delight of crispy baati, rich dal, and sweet churma.

Ingredients:

For Baati (Baked Wheat Dumplings):
2 cups whole wheat flour
1/2 cup semolina (sooji)
1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
Salt to taste
Water (as needed for kneading)
Ghee for brushing
For Dal (Lentil Curry):
1 cup mixed lentils (toor dal, moong dal, chana dal)
1 onion (finely chopped)
2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
2-3 green chilies (finely chopped)
1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
For Churma (Sweet Crumbled Wheat):
1 cup whole wheat flour
1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
1/2 cup powdered jaggery (or as per taste)
1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
Water (as needed for kneading)

Steps:

Baati Preparation:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, semolina, carom seeds, salt, and ghee.
2. Knead the mixture into a stiff dough using water as needed.
3. Divide the dough into equal-sized portions and shape them into round balls.
4. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
5. Place the baati balls on a baking tray and bake for about 35-40 minutes until they turn golden brown and crisp.
6. Remove from the oven and brush with ghee.
Dal Preparation:
1. Rinse the mixed lentils thoroughly and pressure cook with salt until soft.
2. Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
3. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
7. Add cooked lentils along with some water (adjust consistency as desired). Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
Churma Preparation:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour and ghee. Rub the mixture between your palms to form breadcrumbs-like texture.
2. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough.
3. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into small balls or flat discs.
4. Heat a pan and cook the dough discs on low heat until they are cooked through and golden brown.
5. Let them cool slightly, then crumble them into a coarse powder.
6. In a separate pan, melt ghee and add powdered jaggery. Cook until the jaggery melts.
7. Add cardamom powder and the crumbled wheat mixture. Mix well until everything is well combined.
8. Cook for a few more minutes until the churma mixture becomes aromatic.

Bombay style Vada Pav

Mumbai’s favorite spicy potato fritter sandwiched in a soft bun.

Ingredients:

For Potato Vada (Potato Fritters):
4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Few curry leaves, chopped
2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
Salt to taste
Oil for frying
For Pav (Buns):
4 pav buns (white dinner rolls)
Butter for toasting
For Garnish and Serving:
Green chutney
Sweet chutney
Garlic chutney (optional)
Thinly sliced onions
Coriander leaves
Lemon wedges

Steps:

Prepare the Potato Mixture:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
2. Add cumin seeds, chopped green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
3. Add turmeric powder and asafoetida. Mix well.
4. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix everything thoroughly.
5. Cook the mixture for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together.
6. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
Shape and Fry the Vadas:
1. Divide the potato mixture into equal-sized balls and flatten them slightly to form patties.
2. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat for frying.
3. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in the potato patties in batches and fry until they turn golden 4. brown and crispy on all sides.
5. Remove the fried vadas from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
Prepare the Pav:
1. Slit the pav buns horizontally, but not all the way through.
2. Spread some garlic chutney (if using), green chutney, and sweet chutney inside the buns.
3. Heat a griddle or tawa and toast the buns with a little butter until they are lightly golden and crispy.
assemble the Vada Pav:
1. Place a hot vada between the toasted pav buns.
2. Garnish with thinly sliced onions, coriander leaves, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
3. Serve immediately with extra chutneys on the side.

Masala Dosa

A crispy South Indian crepe served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

For Dosa Batter:
1 cup rice
1/4 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
Water, as needed
Salt, to taste
For Potato Filling (Masala):
2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
1 onion, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped
1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
A few curry leaves
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
Salt, to taste
2 tablespoons oil

Steps:

Prepare Dosa Batter:
1. Wash rice and lentils separately, then soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
2. Grind the soaked rice and lentils separately to a smooth paste using enough water. Mix both the pastes together, add salt, and mix well. Ferment the batter overnight or for about 8 hours until it doubles in volume.
Make Potato Filling (Masala):
1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
2. Add chopped onions and green chili. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
3. Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well.
4. Season with salt and cook for a few minutes. Remove from heat and keep aside.
Make Dosas:
1. Heat a non-stick pan or dosa tawa over medium heat. Once hot, pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the pan and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
2. Drizzle a little oil around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom turns golden brown.
3. Spoon some potato filling (masala) onto the center of the dosa and fold it from both sides to make a roll.
4. Cook for another minute or until the dosa is crisp and golden brown.
5. Remove from the pan and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Chole Kulche

A North Indian classic of spicy chickpea curry with fluffy kulchas.

Ingredients:

For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
2 medium-sized onions (finely chopped)
2 medium-sized tomatoes (finely chopped)
2-3 cloves of garlic (minced)
1-inch piece of ginger (minced)
2-3 green chilies (finely chopped)
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons oil
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
For Kulche (Flatbread):
2 cups all-purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 cup yogurt
2 tablespoons oil
Salt to taste
Water (as needed for kneading)

Steps:

Chole Preparation:
1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with salt until soft.
2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
4. Add minced ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
6. Add all the dry spices - coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
7. Add cooked chickpeas along with some water (adjust consistency as desired). Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together.
8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
Kulche Preparation:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, yogurt, and oil.
2. Knead the mixture into a soft dough, adding water gradually if needed.
3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
4. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll them into balls.
5. Roll out each ball into a flatbread (kulcha) using a rolling pin.
6. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat and cook the kulchas on both sides until golden brown spots appear.
7. Optionally, brush the cooked kulchas with some butter or ghee.
